Independence Day

Independence Day in Sri Lanka is celebrated annually on the 4th of February. Sri Lanka gained independence from the British on February 4, 1948. Sri Lanka celebrated its 75th independence year in 2023. February 4th is a national holiday in Sri Lanka. D.S. Senanayake was selected as the first prime minister of Sri Lanka when itgained independence from the British.

Sinhala & Tamil New Year

The Sinhalese and Tamil people of Sri Lanka celebrate the Sinhala and Tamil New Year as a significant cultural event. It's an occasion for friends and family to gather together and celebrate the beginning of a new year. The festival is celebrated on the first day of the month of April, which is the first month of the traditional Sinhala and Tamil years. The new moon's sighting determines the date.

Kandy Esala Perahera

The Kandy Esala Perahera is a grand Buddhist procession held in Kandy. It is one of the most important religious and cultural events in Sri Lanka, and it attracts people from all over the world. The procession takes place over ten nights in July or August, and it features a colourful parade of elephants, dancers, musicians, and other performers. The Temple of the Tooth Relic, a precious Buddhist relic that is said to be the tooth of the Buddha, is the procession's highlight.

Thai Pongal

Thai Pongal is a harvest festival celebrated by Tamils in Sri Lanka. It is one of the most important festivals in the Tamil calendar, and it brings together family and friends to enjoy the harvest. Thai Pongal is celebrated on the first day of the Tamil month of Thai, which is usually in January. The festival is named after the word "Pongal", which is a type of rice dish that is cooked during the festival.

Vesak

Vesak is the most important festival in the Buddhist calendar. Vesak is also known as the festival of light. It celebrates the birth, enlightenment, and death of the Buddha. Vesak is a season of happiness and peace. Buddhists come together and celebrate their faith during the Vesak season. Vesak is celebrated on the full moon in May. Visiting the Temple is an important part of the Vesak Poya Day activities of every Buddhist family.

Deepavali

Deepavali is one of the most important festivals in the Hindu calendar, and it is a time for family and friends to come together and celebrate the victory of good over evil. The festival is a time for Hindus to reflect on the good in the world and to pray for a better future. Deepavali is a public holiday in Sri Lanka. New clothes are worn, and presents are given and received. It is usual to see fireworks during the festival.

Christmas

Christmas in Sri Lanka is a major cultural and religious festival celebrated by Christians and non-Christians alike. The festival is celebrated on December 25th, the same as in other parts of the world. In Sri Lanka, Christmas is a time for family and friends to come together and celebrate the birth of Jesus Christ. People attend church services, exchange gifts, and feast on traditional Sri Lankan Christmas foods. Christmas is not limited to Christian homes only.

Lanka Premier League(LPL)

The Lanka Premier League, which is popularly known as the LPL, is an international T20 cricket league in Sri Lanka. The Sri Lanka Cricket Council started the LPL in 2020, which is the first franchise-based T20 league in Sri Lanka. The LPL is a major event in the Sri Lankan cricket calendar. The LPL is played during the Sri Lankan off-season, from August to September. International players also take part in this tournament.
